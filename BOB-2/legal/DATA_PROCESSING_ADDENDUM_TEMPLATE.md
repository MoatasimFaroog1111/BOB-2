# Data Processing Addendum Template

> For qualified Saudi legal review. Complete the schedules and execute with the main agreement.

This Data Processing Addendum (DPA) forms part of the agreement between **[Customer legal entity]** (Customer) and **[Provider legal entity]** (Provider), effective **[date]**.

## 1. Roles and instructions

For personal data submitted to GuardianAI/BOB-2 by or for Customer, Customer is the controller and Provider is the processor unless a schedule expressly states otherwise. Provider processes data only on documented instructions in the agreement, order form, configured service and authorized support requests, except where law requires other processing.

Customer confirms that its instructions and collection of personal data are lawful and that it has provided required notices and permissions.

## 3. Confidentiality and personnel

Provider ensures persons authorized to process personal data are bound by confidentiality, receive appropriate training and access only what is needed for their duties.

## 4. Security measures

Provider maintains the controls in the Security Schedule, including tenant isolation, least privilege, MFA, encrypted transport, encrypted secret storage, logging, dependency and vulnerability management, backup/restore testing and incident response. Customer remains responsible for user authorization, endpoint security, source-document legality and review of accounting output.

## 6. Assistance with rights requests

Considering the nature of processing, Provider will reasonably assist Customer with verified requests for access, correction, restriction or destruction. Provider will not directly fulfill a request concerning Customer-controlled data unless Customer authorizes it or law requires it.

## 7. Incidents

Provider will notify Customer without undue delay after confirming a personal-data breach affecting Customer data and will provide available information needed for assessment and required notifications. Initial notice may be supplemented as investigation proceeds. Customer is responsible for authority/data-subject notification unless law assigns that duty to Provider.

## 8. Impact assessments and consultations

Provider will provide information reasonably necessary for Customer's privacy impact assessment and regulator consultation, considering available information and the nature of the service. Additional bespoke work may be subject to agreed fees.

## 9. Return, deletion and legal holds

At termination, Provider will make Customer data available for export for **[period]**, then delete or anonymize it from active systems according to Schedule C, except data required by law, tax/accounting retention, security evidence, legal hold or dispute. Backup copies expire through the documented backup lifecycle and remain protected until deletion.

## 10. Audit and evidence

Provider will make current compliance evidence reasonably available, such as security summaries, test results, subprocessor lists and backup/restore evidence. On-site or bespoke audits require prior notice, confidentiality, reasonable scope, no access to other customers' data and reimbursement of exceptional cost unless a material Provider breach is found.

## 11. Cross-border transfers

Provider will not change approved processing locations or transfer mechanisms without documenting the lawful basis, safeguards and any required assessment. Locations and safeguards are listed in Schedule B.

## 12. Liability and precedence

Liability and limitations follow the main agreement unless applicable law requires otherwise. This DPA prevails only for conflicting provisions about personal-data processing.
